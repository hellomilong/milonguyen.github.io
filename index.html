Beyond Labels

Date: 1/11/2024

My life is like a colorful patchwork quilt, knitted together by different pieces that seem out of place and random at first, but together, they create something special. I grew up in a small village in Vietnam, surrounded by rice fields, roosters, ducks, cows, and goats. Those days were innocent and carefree, and I loved every bit of that farm life. It's funny because when people get to know me, it catches them off guard how much I enjoy the farm life. They often have a set image of what an Asian person should be like, and a farmer isn't usually part of that picture. But for me, those early days are precious memories that remind me of a time when life was less complicated.

Then there's another side of me that surprises people. My uncle was a professional boxer, and he trained me in the boxing ring from a young age. I learned to be strong, disciplined, and resilient. Boxing also led me to express myself through tattoos, which is quite common in the boxing world. Each tattoo symbolizes a spiritual realization that I have had, and together, my body tells a story of my personal growth. However, my tattoos often give people the wrong idea about me. When people see my tattoos, they think I'm tough or rebellious. But the truth is, I'm actually very shy and a bit of a nerd. I love math and computer science. These subjects fascinate me, and I can spend hours lost in numbers and codes.

It's strange how people try to fit me into a box based on what they see or expect. But I've learned that we're all more than just one thing. I'm a farmer at heart, a boxer by training, and a nerd by choice. These things do not belong to the same social category, and I'm okay with that. It's like what John Stuart Mill said about individuality. He once stated, "The individual is not accountable to society for his actions, in so far as these concern the interests of no person but himself." This quote reminds me to tune out the noise of social expectations and follow my own heart, and that it's okay to be who I am, even if it doesn't fit neatly into one category or another.

Reflecting on my life, I remember feeling like there was something wrong with me because I didn't fit perfectly into any specific group. But as I grew older, I realized that in order to be happy and live a fulfilling life, I need to stay true to myself and embrace all parts of me. It's okay to be different, to be a mix of many things. In the end, my story shows that we shouldn't judge someone based on one aspect, one mistake, or one accomplishment of their life. My journey has taught me to appreciate this complexity in myself and in others. We're all more than just labels and categories, and thatâ€™s what makes the world so exciting and diverse.
